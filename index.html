About Me

I’m Oskar Przybylski /pʃɪˈbɪlski/

he/him

I grew up in Poland but found myself living in London, a city that now feels like a natural habitat to me, with its vibrance and melting pot of cultures.

I am a keen hiker and traveler, always in search of new adventures. Music (I play guitar) and skateboarding are other lifelong passions of mine. When I'm occasionally stuck at home, I tend to geek out on whatever topic sparks my interest, and more recently, I've been diving into coding.

What truly captivates me across all my hobbies, though, are the people I meet and their stories. I am on a never-ending journey of learning and understanding the world around us, with the aim to create a positive impact for my community and the people I encounter.

Currently, I work as an acoustic engineer within the architectural engineering sector. I believe that design and creation of architecture is the ultimate exercise in human collaboration. It requires seamless cooperation of countless disciplines, be it mechanical and structural engineers, sustainibility consultants, designers, architects and finally contractors and builders. I am proud that my work focuses on making our built environment a bit better and more sustainable for the public it serves. Though I have made the decision that it is time for a change and I look towards coding, I intend to seek a path that allows me to keep aligned with my principles.

Where I Am Now

Coming from the field of architecture, I have always been passionate about building stuff. Seeing projects evolve from an idea, taking shape on a drawing board, and ultimately materializing into physical structures that serve people is what has been really exciting to me about my work.

I have started learning coding as a way to make my job easier and automate some of the more daunting tasks using Python. I quickly realized that there is so much more in it for me than I thought and that I have lots of fun working through coding problems and building my own projects. In a way, this lets me be a designer, architect, engineer, and builder all at once.

This, combined with realizing some of the limitations of my current career path, naturally led me to the decision to switch careers and pursue coding as my next step. This is when I learned about Founders and Coders, and after doing my research, I have decided that this is the right place to help me with that goal.

Where I Want To Be

Entering the world of coding felt like stepping into a universe of endless possibilities. I have realized just how much impact one can create at the intersection of the digital and physical realms. With awareness of new technologies emerging rapidly and their profound implications, in my future work, I want to continue to provide positive value to society and help build software and services that enhance the lives of people from all walks of life.

Switching to a coding career also offers the advantage of granting me greater independence in choosing where I want to live in the future, something that is very important to me, given my restless nature.

However, I would not consider it if coding was not simply really fun to me. Previously, I never identified myself as a "computer guy", steering towards other interests on the boundary of creative and technical fields. Yet, it just shows that trying completely new things can bring some pretty surprising self-discoveries.
